<br>
After graduating from high school on July 1st 2022, I studied Business & Management at the University of Trento (Italian: Università di Trento) for only one year: from September 2022 to June 2023.
<br>
For many reasons, that I won't list here and now, I decided to pursue a substantially different bachelor's degree abroad.
<br>
In August 2023, I moved to Copenhagen (Danish: København), the capital city of Denmark (Danish: Danmark), to start my studies in Data Science at the IT University of Copenhagen (Danish: IT Universitetet i København).
<br>
<br>
During the intro week (yes, you understood that right: an entire week dedicated to introducing you to the most important aspects to know before starting your academic journey), I could immediately feel the main difference compared to my previous experience: the attention to the individual student. The following semesters confirmed my initial intuition.
<br>
The lectures are mainly theoretical, with some practical examples to make the learning easier: comparable to what I was used to. However, most courses also have exercise classes and projects. This was one of the aspects that made me applied for this program: I was looking for a good balance between theory and practice, something I couldn't find before.
<br>
The professors I’ve had so far are quite young (I’d say with an average age of 40), and this, together with the fact that English does not have a formal courtesy form, reduces the barrier between lecturers and students making their interactions more relaxed.
<br>
Regarding the exercise classes, they are led by students who have already taken the course in previous years and are there primarily to provide support if needed.
<br>
Overall, I consider the quality of teaching satisfactory, with the usual highs and lows.
<br>
You’re probably wondering where the "attention to the individual student" lies, as I haven’t really explained it so far.
